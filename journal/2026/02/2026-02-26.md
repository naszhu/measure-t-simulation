From PMDC literature, we know:

- Importance manipulations affect thresholds: Important PM → lower PM threshold, higher ongoing threshold ("selective delay") [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/326878878_Racing_to_Remember_A_Theory_of_Decision_Control_in_Event-Based_Prospective_Memory)
- Focal/nonfocal affects threshold magnitude: Nonfocal shows larger threshold increases than focal [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/326878878_Racing_to_Remember_A_Theory_of_Decision_Control_in_Event-Based_Prospective_Memory)
- Drift rates reflect capacity sharing OR reactive inhibition on PM trials [Sage Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1747021820914915)[ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/326878878_Racing_to_Remember_A_Theory_of_Decision_Control_in_Event-Based_Prospective_Memory)
- Individual differences in PM reflect strategic control (threshold) vs capacity limits (drift) [PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5796868/)[ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/315815158_Accumulating_Evidence_About_What_Prospective_Memory_Costs_Actually_Reveal)

**"Do value-based selection mechanisms in MIS operate through the same cognitive control architecture as PM intention retrieval?"**

1. **If MIS importance = PMDC importance** (both are value-based prioritization):
    - PM importance manipulation should affect MCP response thresholds
    - High-reward MCP responses should have lower thresholds
    - This predicts: PM-important blocks show altered MCP threshold patterns

    - Nonfocal PM (requires monitoring) should reduce MCP drift rates
    - Focal PM (no monitoring) should NOT reduce MCP drift rates
    - This distinguishes: Is MIS capacity "monitoring" or something else?
3. **Individual differences**:
    - People who show strong threshold control in PM should show strong importance effects in MIS
    - People who show capacity limitations in PM should show drift rate reductions in MIS
    - This reveals: What cognitive constructs do MIS parameters index?
