---

- An ‚Äú[[event]]‚Äù = one unfolding of the process (one ball drop, one minute of arrivals).
    
- A ‚Äú[[distribution]]‚Äù = the accumulated pattern across many possible unfoldings.
    
- ‚Äú[[expectation]]‚Äù = the long-run balance point of that pattern.
    
- ‚Äú[[variance]] ‚Äù = the long-run spread of that pattern.

- A distribution is the **shape of possibility** generated by a repeatable process.
    
- Different distributions correspond to different assumptions about how that process unfolds in time or structure.
    
- Expectation and variance are _summary handles_ on that shape, not independent things.
