## **Course Title:** Logical Writing and Argumentation

### **Course Description**

This course develops the use of logic in writing. Students learn how to construct clear, persuasive arguments, organize ideas coherently, and avoid faulty reasoning. Emphasis is placed on argument structure, evidence, clarity, and revision.

---

## **Learning Objectives**

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

- Construct clear, logically structured arguments
    
- Develop strong thesis statements and supporting claims
    
- Use evidence effectively and responsibly
    
- Identify and avoid logical fallacies
    
- Revise writing for clarity, coherence, and precision
    
- Critically evaluate arguments in texts
    

---

## **Course Structure (Weekly Topics)**

### **Week 1: What Is Logical Writing?**

- Writing as structured thinking
    
- Claims, reasons, and conclusions
    
- Difference between opinion and argument
    

### **Week 2: Building Strong Claims**

- Thesis statements
    
- Arguable vs. descriptive claims
    
- Precision and scope
    

### **Week 3: Argument Structure**

- Premises and conclusions
    
- Basic argument patterns
    
- Paragraph-level logic
    

### **Week 4: Coherence and Flow**

- Logical sequencing of ideas
    
- Transitions and signposting
    
- Avoiding jumps in reasoning
    

### **Week 5: Evidence and Support**

- Types of evidence (examples, data, authority)
    
- Relevance and sufficiency
    
- Integrating sources into writing
    

### **Week 6: Counterarguments**

- Anticipating objections
    
- Refutation strategies
    
- Strengthening arguments through opposition
    

### **Week 7: Logical Fallacies I**

- Common fallacies (straw man, ad hominem, false dilemma)
    
- Why they are persuasive but flawed
    

### **Week 8: Logical Fallacies II**

- Slippery slope, circular reasoning, hasty generalization
    
- Identifying fallacies in real texts
    

### **Week 9: Clarity and Precision**

- Eliminating ambiguity
    
- Avoiding vague language
    
- Definitions and conceptual clarity
    

### **Week 10: Style and Logical Emphasis**

- Sentence structure and emphasis
    
- Parallelism and balance
    
- Concision vs. completeness
    

### **Week 11: Persuasion and Rhetoric**

- Logical appeal (logos) vs. emotional/ethical appeals
    
- Audience awareness
    
- Framing arguments effectively
    

### **Week 12: Revision as Logical Refinement**

- Diagnosing weak arguments
    
- Strengthening structure and support
    
- Peer review
    

### **Week 13: Writing Workshop**

- Drafting and feedback
    
- Individual conferences
    

### **Week 14: Final Presentations / Portfolio Submission**

---

## **Assignments**

- **Short Argument Exercises (20%)**
    
- **Analytical Essay (20%)** – Evaluate someone else’s argument
    
- **Argumentative Essay (30%)** – Develop your own sustained argument
    
- **Revision Portfolio (20%)**
    
- **Participation / Peer Review (10%)**
    

---

## **Key Concepts Emphasized**

- Claim → Reason → Evidence → Conclusion
    
- Coherence and progression
    
- Logical consistency
    
- Audience-aware reasoning
